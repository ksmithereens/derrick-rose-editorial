Derrick Rose

An NBA Journey

Chapter 1: Mountain Top

2008-2009 : Rookie of the Year

Rose was selected with the first overall pick in the 2008 draft by the Chicago Bulls. Rose won Rookie of the Year, joining Jordan (1985) and Elton Brand (2000) as the only Bulls to do so

2010-2011: MVP

At the end of the 2010–11 NBA season the Bulls finished with a league leading record of 62–20. On May 3rd, Rose was named the NBA Most Valuable Player, joining Jordan as the only players to receive the award in Chicago Bulls history. At 22 years and 6 months old, Rose also became the youngest player to receive the award.

Chapter 2: Rock Bottom

2011-2012 : ACL Tear

During Game 1 of the first round of the playoffs against the Philadelphia 76ers, Rose injured his left knee while trying to jump. He was immediately helped off the court.

An MRI later revealed that Rose tore the ACL in his left knee and would miss the rest of the playoffs. Rose had surgery performed on May 12, 2012, with an estimated recovery period of 8–12 months.

Chapter 3: Ups And Downs

2013–14 season : Return and Torn Meniscus

After missing the entire 2012-2013 season, his first official game was in a 107–95 loss against the defending champions Miami Heat on October 29th. Rose was limited to 12 points in 34 minutes of play.

On November 22nd, Rose injured his right knee during a game against the Portland Trail Blazers. An MRI confirmed Rose tore his right knee meniscus and that surgery was required. Rose underwent surgery and the Bulls announced that he was out for the season.

2014-2015: Back To The Playoffs

Rose returned from injury to play in the Bulls' season opener against the New York Knicks on October 29th, and recorded 13 points and 5 assists in 21 minutes. He then scored a season-high 32 points on January 14 against the Washington Wizards, before being ruled out again with another knee injury a month later.

He appeared in 51 games, the most he'd played since the 2010–11 season.

On February 24th, Rose reinjured the same meniscus and was scheduled to miss just four to six weeks. Rose returned to action on April 8th, playing just 19 minutes. On April 18th, Rose played in his first playoff game since tearing his ACL in the 2012 Playoffs. Rose finished with 23 points and 7 assists. On May 8th, Rose hit a buzzer-beater three and scored 30 points to give the Bulls a 99–96 victory over Cleveland and a 2–1 lead in the Eastern Conference Semis. The Cavaliers won the final three games to take the series in six games.

2015-2016: Final Season With The Bulls

A preseason left orbital bone fracture saw Rose begin the regular season wearing a face mask. On November 5th, Rose scored a then season-high 29 points in a 104–98 win over the Oklahoma City Thunder. He showed signs of his old MVP self as he scored 10 points over the final three and a half minutes to lift the Bulls. He continued playing well throughout the season.

Chapter 4: New Scenery

2016-2017: New York Knicks

On June 22nd, Rose was traded to the New York Knicks.

He made his debut for the Knicks in the team's season opener on October 25th against the Cleveland Cavaliers. In 29 minutes of action, he scored 17 points in a 117–88 loss.

He was playing pretty well, until April 2nd when he was ruled out for the rest of the season after tearing the meniscus in his left knee.

Chapter 5: Deep Thoughts

2017-2018: Cleveland Cavaliers

On July 25th, Rose signed with the Cleveland Cavaliers. In his debut for the Cavaliers against the Boston Celtics on October 17th, Rose scored 14 points in a 102–99 win.

In late November, Rose left the team to re-evaluate his future in the NBA. His increasing frustration with injuries caused him to question his desire to continue playing.

He returned to working with the Cavaliers' medical staff in early December in hopes of recovering from a sprained left ankle and bone spurs.

On January 18th, Rose returned after missing more than two months with ankle injuries and scored nine points in 13 minutes in a win over the Orlando Magic.

Chapter 6 : Resilience

2018–2019: Minnesota Timberwolves

On February 8th, Rose was acquired by the Utah Jazz in a three-team trade. Two days later, he was waived by the Jazz.

In early March, Rose signed with the Minnesota Timberwolves, reuniting him with Tom Thibodeau, Jimmy Butler and Taj Gibson.

During the playoffs, Rose averaged 14.2 points in 23.8 minutes per game, as the Timberwolves lost 4–1 to the Houston Rockets in the first round.

On July 4th, Rose re-signed with the Timberwolves for the 2018–19 season. On October 31th (Halloween), Rose scored a career-high 50 points in a win over the Utah Jazz. This was a very emotional game for Rose as it proved that he still had some game left in him

Right ankle trouble saw Rose miss 11 of 19 games from December to January. He missed an extra three games in early February. On March 21th, he was ruled out for the rest of the season with a right elbow injury. Rose ended the season with 18.0 points and 4.3 assists per game with a career-high 37% field goal percentage for three pointers. However, the Timberwolves missed the playoffs.

Chapter 7: Still Kickin'

2019-2021: Detroit Pistons

On July 7th, Rose signed with the Detroit Pistons.

On October 23th, he made his debut for the Pistons, logging 18 points, three rebounds, and nine assists in a win over the Indiana Pacers. Rose became the first player in Pistons history to record seven consecutive 20+ point games as a reserve.

The streak ended after he suffered a groin injury during a game with the Denver Nuggets. During the month of January when he accomplished the feat, Rose averaged 22 points, six assists and three rebounds.

In his third game after returning from injury, Rose scored a season-high 31 points en route to a win against the Phoenix Suns. Despite a strong individual season by Rose as the team's sixth man, the Pistons ended the season as 13th place in the Eastern Conference with a disappointing 20-46 record.

Chapter 8: Back to New York

2021-2023: Return To The Knicks

On February 8th, Rose was traded back to the New York Knicks, once again reuniting him with coach Tom Thibodeau and former teammate Taj Gibson.

Rose helped the Knicks finish fourth in the East and placed third in the Sixth Man of the Year voting.

In late May, in his first playoff game since 2018, Rose had 17 points, five assists and five rebounds in a loss to the Atlanta Hawks 107–105. In Game 2, he scored a game-high 26 points, in a 101–92 win to even the series. On May 28th, he recorded a season-high 30 point in a 105–94 loss as Atlanta took a 2–1 series lead. The Hawks won the series 4–1, as Rose suffered a knee injury at the end of the series.

Later, in August, the Knicks extended Rose's contract by three years with a $43 million deal.

Rose suffered a right ankle injury on December 17th and was ruled out for two months. Two months later, Rose had surgery for a skin infection on his ankle and was ruled out indefinitely.

On June 24, 2023, the Knicks declined Rose's team option for the 2023–24 season, making him a free agent.

Chapter 9: Home Sweet Home

2023-Present: Memphis Grizzlies

On July 3rd, Rose signed with the Memphis Grizzlies. He chose to wear the number 23, the same number he wore when playing for the University of Memphis.

He received an ovation in his debut from Memphis fans on October 26th, as he scored 8 points in 16 minutes in a 111–104 loss to the New Orleans Pelicans. On November 24th, Rose scored a season-high 17 points in a 110–89 loss to the Phoenix Suns.
